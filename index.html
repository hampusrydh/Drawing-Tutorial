Get a fundemental understanding of art by copying

To copy or imitate is not creative, yet is one of the greatest methods of improvment to enhance your drawing skills. It teaches you to understand the proportions and materials, which is vital at least for any vaguely realistic style of art. But, for this to work, you need to pay attention and have a good reference! Be careful with your pencil strokes and frequently take a step back to see what effects they have on the larger image. In almost all artforms, even small changes can have a big and unpredictable impact on the apperance of the work. The tiniest change in angle or curvature could make just as big of a difference as would spilling your cup of coffee over the whole thing, and can be just as hard to undo if you are not in luck. Most things do not work like they seem!

Drawing a Realistic Ice Cube

For the purposes of this tutorial, I encourage you to follow my own drawing closely. But if you think you can manage with more freedom, try!


Step 1 - Preparation

This step is often overlooked, even by yours truly, but should at least be given some consideration to avoid disaster. Find some good references, a comfortable location, good lighting and a flat surface without excess dust or breadcrums. Get ready one or a few pieces of paper, pencils of varying hardness, a sharpener and a good eraser. If you only have bad erasers that smudge out everything, just forget it and bring more papers and patience.


Step 2 - Planning and contour

This is the most important step - proceed with caution. Decide where to put the visible corners of the ice cube and carefully mark them out. Trying to keep track of the hidden corners as well could be useful for increased accuracy, but is highly difficult and confusing. As such, I have decided to avoid it. If it looks good, gently draw lines connecting them. If it looks a little off, try first to pinpoint the error and then experiment by slightly adjusting the position of the points until it looks good. If you are having trouble, experiment on another piece of paper, draw out the lines, compare with referneces and consider the possibility that it does in fact not look all that bad.


When you draw the lines, you can add the bumps, curves and imperfections characteriztic to the shape of an ice cube immedeately if you wish, rather than doing it later, as it follows what you might expect quite closely. And do not forget to round the corners! The word "ice cube" is misleading.



Form a few unique areas where the shade, shape or texture will differ notably. Begin with large shapes like the dark areas (often located at the center) and highlights (often going along edges in quite thin strips). Move on to cracks and areas with many things going on. Rather than trying to add small reflective details everywhere, make areas for them and add a few small extra details afterwards.
